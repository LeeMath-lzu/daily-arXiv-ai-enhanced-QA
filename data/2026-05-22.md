### [1] [Shading A-polynomials via huge representations of $U_q(\mathfrak{su}_N)$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22560)
*Dmitry Galakhov,Alexei Morozov*

Main category: hep-th

TL;DR: 本文提出了SU(N)李群经典阴影A-多项式的构造方法，利用Clebsh-Gordan弦形式体系，将SU(2)情况下的A-多项式推广到任意李(超)代数，并在双缩放极限下讨论了量子A-多项式的推广可能，给出了su₃情况下几个具体纽结的显式例子。

Motivation: 经典A-多项式在SU(2)情形已完善定义，但扩展到更高秩李群（如SU(N)）的A-多项式构造尚不明确。作者希望利用CG弦形式体系，建立一般化框架以推广到任意李(超)代数。

Method: 采用Clebsh-Gordan弦形式体系，结合WZW型二维CFT或量子群U_q(su_N)的语言，考虑双缩放极限（q=e^ℏ, ℏ→0且表示权重巨大），并参考纽结接触同调中的 augmentation 多项式概念。

Result: 成功构造了SU(N)的经典阴影A-多项式A_a(l_b,m_c)，并给出了su₃下纽结3₁、4₁和5₁的显式A-多项式例子。

Conclusion: CG弦形式体系可自然推广到任意su_N及任意表示，有望帮助推导任意李(超)代数g的量子A-多项式。

Abstract: Classical A-polynomials $A(\ell,m)$ define constraints on coordinates $\ell$ and $m$ in $SL(2,\mathbb{C})$ (a complexification of $SU(2)$) character varieties associated to knot complements $S^3\setminus K$. Quantum A-polynomials $\hat A(\hat \ell,\hat m)$ are difference operators annihilating Jones polynomials believed to represent wave functions of 3d Chern-Simons theory with gauge group $SU(2)$ on a toroidal pipe surrounding the knot $K$ strand -- a boundary of the knot complements $S^3\setminus K$. We suggest a construction of classical shaded A-polynomials $A_a(\ell_b,m_c)$ associated to Lie groups $SU(N)$. We exploit a formalism of Clebsh-Gordan (CG) chords, where indices $a$, $b$, $c$ run over $1,\ldots,N-1$. CG chords have a natural interpretation in terms of 2d CFTs of WZW type, or, alternatively, in terms of quantum group $U_q(\mathfrak{su}_N)$. In the case of $\mathfrak{su}_2$ CG chords could be associated to Reeb chords in a knot contact homology (KCH) framework. KCH suggests its own analogue of A-polynomials known as augmentation polynomials allowed to have extra spurious roots in principle. Yet the CG chord formalism could be easily extended to arbitrary $\mathfrak{su}_N$ allowing us to generalize the construction of A(ugmentation)-polynomials to arbitrary $\mathfrak{su}_N$ and arbitrary representation as well. Primarily we aim at classical A-polynomials by considering a double scaling limit when $q=e^{\hbar}$, $\hbar\to 0$ and the representations are huge, in particular, highest weight vector components $w_i\to \infty$ so that $\hbar w_i\sim m_i$ remain finite. Still we expect the presented techniques would be helpful in deriving quantum A-polynomials for arbitrary Lie (super)algebras $\mathfrak{g}$. Also we discuss explicit examples of A-polynomials for knots $3_1$, $4_1$ and $5_1$ for $\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{su}_3$.
