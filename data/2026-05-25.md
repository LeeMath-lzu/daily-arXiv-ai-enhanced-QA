### [1] [A lower bound for the genus of a knot using the Links-Gould invariant](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.15617)
*Ben-Michael Kohli,Guillaume Tahar*

Main category: math.GT

TL;DR: 本文利用量子群$U_{q}\mathfrak{gl}(2 \vert 1)$的表示理论，证明Links-Gould多项式$LG^{2,1}$的次数给出了任何纽结的Seifert亏格的下界，改进了Alexander多项式的Seifert不等式。该不等式能检测到Kinoshita-Terasaka纽结和Conway纽结的亏格≥2，而经典工具（Alexander多项式、Levine-Tristram符号差）无法做到。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 已有的Alexander多项式及其Seifert不等式在检测某些纽结（如Kinoshita-Terasaka和Conway纽结）的亏格时能力不足，需要寻找更强大的不变量来提供更紧的亏格下界。

Method: 利用量子群$U_{q}\mathfrak{gl}(2 \vert 1)$的表示理论，研究Links-Gould多项式$LG^{2,1}$的性质，证明其次数可提供纽结Seifert亏格的下界。

Result: Links-Gould多项式的次数给出了任何纽结的Seifert亏格的下界，改进了Alexander多项式的Seifert不等式。对于Kinoshita-Terasaka和Conway纽结，该下界为≥2，而Alexander多项式和Levine-Tristram符号差无法给出此信息。

Conclusion: Links-Gould多项式是一个比Alexander多项式更强大的亏格下界工具，能够检测到某些经典不变量无法区分的纽结的亏格信息。

Abstract: The Links-Gould invariant of links $LG^{2,1}$ is a two-variable generalization of the Alexander-Conway polynomial. Using representation theory of $U_{q}\mathfrak{gl}(2 \vert 1)$, we prove that the degree of the Links-Gould polynomial provides a lower bound on the Seifert genus of any knot, therefore improving the bound known as the Seifert inequality in the case of the Alexander polynomial. As an example, unlike some classical tools such as the Alexander polynomial and Levine-Tristram signature, this new genus bound detects the fact that the Kinoshita-Terasaka and Conway knots have genus greater or equal to 2.

Main category: math.RT

TL;DR: 本文通过仿射Hecke代数的Langlands/Zelevinsky参数化，将Frenkel-Reshetikhin q-特征的主要部分与自然不变量对应起来，并引入互反特征概念，证明了在量子仿射Schur-Weyl对偶下与主q-特征的匹配关系，实现了Nakajima关于q-特征中主导单项式在Lusztig框架中作用的期望。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Result: 证明了互反特征与主导q-特征在量子仿射Schur-Weyl对偶下匹配；在仿射Hecke范畴化$U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_\infty)^+$的框架下，互反特征是单项式基坐标在q=1时的特化；主导q-特征的重数由单项式基与典范基（或PBW基）之间的过渡系数描述。

Conclusion: 建立了仿射Hecke代数与量子仿射代数之间的深层联系，为q-特征理论提供了组合解释，并给出了计算主导重数的显式公式。

Abstract: We identify the dominant part of the Frenkel-Reshetikhin $q$-character with a natural invariant arising from the Langlands/Zelevinsky parameterization for affine Hecke algebras.

The main theorem claims that the reciprocal character matches, under quantum affine Schur--Weyl duality, with the dominant $q$-character for finite-dimensional modules over quantum affine algebras.
  This result gives a type $A$ realization of the Nakajima expectation that the dominant monomials in the $q$-character should play the role of monomial-basis coordinates in Lusztig's framework for finite quantum groups.
