### [1] [Spectrally Corrected Polynomial Approximation for Quantum Singular Value Transformation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03998)
*Krishnan Suresh*

Main category: quant-ph

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一种基于量子奇异值变换（QSVT）的谱修正方法，利用矩阵的部分已知特征值信息来改进线性方程组的量子求解精度，显著降低了电路深度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统QSVT方法使用连续区间上的多项式逼近1/x函数，电路深度与矩阵条件数平方根成正比，且未利用矩阵的任何特性。作者观察到QSVT的求解精度实际上只取决于多项式在矩阵特征值处的逼近精度，因此可以利用已知特征值信息来提高效率。

Method: 提出谱修正方法：给定任意基多项式p0（如Remez多项式），通过求解K×K线性方程组，强制多项式在K个已知特征值处精确插值1/x函数，而不增加多项式次数。修正后的多项式p_SC保持特征值间的连续误差分布，并继承基多项式的奇偶性。

Result: 在一维泊松方程上的QSVT实验显示，相对于基多项式，电路深度减少了5倍，同时保持单位保真度并改善了合规误差。修正方法对基多项式选择不敏感，对特征值扰动（相对误差高达10%）具有鲁棒性。扩展到二维泊松方程表明，修正一小部分谱可能就足以实现0.999以上的保真度。

Conclusion: 谱修正方法通过利用矩阵的部分特征值知识，显著提高了QSVT求解线性方程组的效率，为量子算法设计提供了新的优化途径，特别是在处理具有已知谱特性的问题时具有重要应用价值。

Abstract: Quantum Singular Value Transformation (QSVT) provides a unified framework for applying polynomial functions to the singular values of a block-encoded matrix. QSVT prepares a state proportional to $\bA^{-1}\bb$ with circuit depth $O(d\cdot\mathrm{polylog}(N))$, where $d$ is the polynomial degree of the $1/x$ approximation and $N$ is the size of $\bA$. Current polynomial approximation methods are over the continuous interval $[a,1]$, giving $d = O(\sqrt{\kap}\log(1/\varepsilon))$, and make no use of any properties of $\bA$.
  We observe here that QSVT solution accuracy depends only on the polynomial accuracy at the eigenvalues of $\bA$. When all $N$ eigenvalues are known exactly, a pure spectral polynomial $p_{S}$ can interpolate $1/x$ at these eigenvalues and achieve unit fidelity at reduced degree. But its practical applicability is limited. To address this, we propose a spectral correction that exploits prior knowledge of $K$ eigenvalues of $\bA$. Given any base polynomial $p_0$, such as Remez, of degree $d_0$, a $K\times K$ linear system enforces exact interpolation of $1/x$ only at these $K$ eigenvalues without increasing $d_0$. The spectrally corrected polynomial $p_{SC}$ preserves the continuous error profile between eigenvalues and inherits the parity of $p_0$.
  QSVT experiments on the 1D Poisson equation demonstrate up to a $5\times$ reduction in circuit depth relative to the base polynomial, at unit fidelity and improved compliance error. The correction is agnostic to the choice of base polynomial and robust to eigenvalue perturbations up to $10\%$ relative error. Extension to the 2D Poisson equation suggests that correcting a small fraction of the spectrum may suffice to achieve fidelity above $0.999$.

  More precisely, let $X$ be a resolution of a Kleinian singularity (for example, $X = T^\ast\mathbb{P}^1$) and let $Z$ be the exceptional divisor.
